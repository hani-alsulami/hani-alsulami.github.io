About

I lead the SOC inside the corporate cybersecurity function of a Saudi digital bank — the team responsible for detecting, triaging, and responding to threats around the clock. My background is in digital forensics and incident response, and I bring that same evidence-first discipline to how the team is built, staffed, and measured.

Day to day that means owning detection strategy, incident command during major events, playbook and use-case development, and translating what the SOC sees into decisions the wider business can act on.

Outside of work, I publish breakdowns of emerging CVEs and forensic techniques for the wider security community — the kind of notes I wish existed when I was coming up through this field.

Threat Hunting

T1055Process Injection

Masquerading svchost.exe child processes

HypothesisAdversaries spawn or inject into svchost.exe to blend malicious activity with normal Windows service noise.
Data sourceSysmon Event ID 1/8, EDR process tree, Windows Security 4688
MethodBaseline expected svchost parent/child relationships and flag deviations, unsigned modules, and unexpected network-capable children.
Validated · detections deployed
T1140Deobfuscate/Decode

Payloads hidden inside PSCredential objects

HypothesisEncrypted PSCredential fields are being repurposed to smuggle PowerShell payloads past plain-text command inspection.
Data sourcePowerShell Script Block Logging (4104), module logging
MethodHunt for PSCredential construction patterns followed by decryption + immediate invoke/execute calls in the same block.
Validated · detections deployed
T1219Remote Access Software

Unauthorized AnyDesk / remote-tool sessions

HypothesisLegitimate remote-access tools are being used off-hours or outside approved change windows as a foothold mechanism.
Data sourceAnyDesk connection trace / ad.trace, firewall egress logs, asset inventory
MethodCross-reference remote-tool session artifacts against approved maintenance windows and known operator IDs.
Validated · detections deployed
T1210Exploitation of Remote Services

HypothesisInternet-facing Exchange servers are being probed for known privilege-escalation and info-disclosure chains ahead of patch availability.
Data sourceIIS/OWA access logs, Exchange HTTP proxy logs, EDR on Exchange hosts
